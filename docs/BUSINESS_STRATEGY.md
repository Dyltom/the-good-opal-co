# Business and Growth Strategy

## Positioning

The Good Opal Co is a founder-led Australian direct-to-consumer opal studio. Its strongest position is not generic luxury jewellery. It is an approachable, detail-rich way to buy an individual Australian opal with personal help available before purchase.

The durable promise is: **see the stone clearly, understand what is known about it, and get help choosing without pressure.**

## Primary customers

1. **Meaningful gift buyer** — wants a distinctive piece and reassurance about colour, delivery, returns, and presentation.
2. **First-time opal buyer** — attracted to opal but uncertain about types, origin, treatments, care, and fair value.
3. **Collector or enthusiast** — compares origin, body tone, weight, dimensions, pattern, condition, and photography.
4. **Custom client** — begins with a stone, occasion, budget, or loose idea and needs a guided brief.

## Current revenue model

- One-of-a-kind finished jewellery and loose opals sold online.
- Custom jewellery enquiries converted through a guided consultation and quote.
- Newsletter-led repeat purchasing when new pieces become available.

The site should optimize for trust and qualified conversations, not artificial urgency or constant discounting. Scarcity is inherent because inventory is individual; it does not need to be manufactured.

## Revenue expansion, in priority order

### 1. Gift concierge

Offer a short guided enquiry by recipient, occasion, budget, colour, and delivery date. Convert uncertain high-intent visitors without building a large new product system.

### 2. Collector drops and private previews

Segment newsletter subscribers by interest and give collectors early access to coherent small releases. Measure repeat purchase rate and revenue per release.

### 3. Virtual viewing and sourcing service

Turn existing product questions into booked video inspections. For qualified budgets, offer a paid or deposit-backed sourcing brief that can be credited toward purchase.

### 4. Custom commissions

Add structured quote stages, deposit invoices, progress updates, and final balance collection after the current enquiry volume proves the workflow. Do not promise fixed timelines before studio capacity is known.

### 5. Care, resizing, and repair

Offer post-purchase care, inspection, resizing, and repair where operational capability exists. This improves lifetime value and creates a reason to return after purchase.

### 6. Education products

Use free articles and buying guides first. Launch paid workshops or courses only after instructor, curriculum, dates, capacity, fulfilment, cancellation policy, and payment flow are real. Never hard-code enrolments, ratings, or availability.

### 7. Trade and partnership sales

Explore small wholesale parcels, jewellery-designer sourcing, gallery partnerships, and corporate gifting after inventory and fulfilment reporting are reliable.

## Conversion principles

- Lead with real product photography and individual product facts.
- Show price, stock, origin, material, dimensions, shipping, and returns near the decision.
- Preserve visitor intent from every CTA into contact forms.
- Use one currency convention internally: catalogue and CMS prices are AUD dollars; Stripe receives integer cents.
- Keep checkout available to guests. Accounts are secondary to order tracking.
- Use discounts only when a real campaign, margin rule, expiry, redemption limit, and Stripe implementation exist.
- Treat unsupported sourcing, certification, warranty, delivery, and popularity claims as launch blockers.

## Production facts still requiring owner confirmation

Before public launch, confirm and place these values in one approved source:

- Legal business name, ABN, registered location, and return address.
- Production domain and verified sending domain.
- Public contact channel and response expectations.
- Exact sourcing language that can be substantiated.
- Which products include certificates and what each certificate represents.
- Carrier, insurance, signature, international destination, and dispatch policies.
- Custom commission deposits, cancellation terms, lead times, and repair capability.
- Verified social profiles, if they should be published.

Until confirmed, the product uses conservative copy and omits the claim rather than guessing.

## Core metrics

- Product view → add-to-cart rate.
- Cart → secure checkout rate.
- Checkout completion rate and payment failures.
- Custom enquiry completion and enquiry → quote → deposit conversion.
- Virtual viewing request → purchase conversion.
- Revenue per available piece and days to sell.
- Newsletter confirmed opt-in, click-through, repeat purchase, and unsubscribe rate.
- Refund, damage, fulfilment delay, and inventory exception rate.
